About Me

Denise St. Jean is a health strategy expert and Project Leader at Boston Consulting Group, where she advises biopharma, biotech, and public sector clients on evidence generation, pipeline planning, and R&D strategy. Her career bridges quantitative research and real-world application—shaped by nearly a decade of work in epidemiology, clinical trials, and health systems innovation.

Denise earned her PhD in Epidemiology (with a concentration in Infectious Diseases) from the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, Gillings School of Global Public Health where she was a Flare Capital Scholar and Robert Wood Johnson Foundation Health Policy Research Scholar. Her dissertation applied advanced causal inference methods to clinical trial and demographic survey data to estimate real-world effectiveness of oral rotavirus vaccine across settings. Her research has been published in Vaccine, Epidemiology & Infection, and Journal of Infectious Diseases. She was recognized by the Journal of Public Health Management and Practice as a “Student Who Rocked Public Health” for her research to understand COVID-19 vaccine hesitancy.

Prior to earning her PhD, Denise was an Analyst at Beghou Consulting and earned her BS from Yale University where she investigated the spread of non-disseminating strains of Lyme disease and Babesiosis as an Edward A. Bouchet Research Fellow. She also holds an MS in Management from Wake Forest University where she was a PepsiCo Corporate Fellow. Across these roles, she has built a reputation for translating rigorous evidence into actionable strategies that advance population health.
